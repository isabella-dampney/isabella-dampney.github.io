HOMEPAGE

My learning plan while at Dev Academy


What is your long term goal and/or career pathway?

I trained and practise as a visual artist, and this is my primary vocation. However, this is a low-paying job that requires a secondary income. After years of working in low-wage jobs to support myself, my long-term goal is to find a job in tech where I can support myself, it has some flexibility about working between the home/office, is ethical as much as possible, and it is engaging.


What non-technical human skills would you like to see developed in yourself while at EDA?

I would love to develop my collaboration skills. In my art practise I also work collaboratively, but often with the same person. I would love to develop my team work skills to work better in small teams with people whom I might not know very well. In my current line of work this is not something I have been able to build on much since leaving university.

I am also enjoying discovering how I learn when it’s a completely new skill. I would like to understand better my habits and holdbacks when it comes to learning and how I manage my energy. Specifically, I would like to develop better faith in myself when it comes to problem solving, as I struggle with self-doubt a lot


Describe your strengths and limitations.

I would say one of my strengths is motivation. I am a very motivated person, and when I would like something to happen I know it takes hard work to get there and I am willing to put in the time.

Related to this is also one of my limitations. I struggle a lot with focus, which can go in both directions. When I am engaged by a subject I can do it for hours on end and do a deep dive but can often get caught in the weeds and before I realise I am completely off track. The flip side of this, is when there is something I don’t want to do I can be very stubborn and just avoid it for days.


A commitment to how will you manage your workload in this programme.

I am comfortable working to tight deadlines. I don’t like leaving things to the last minute and prefer the slow and steady approach. It doesn’t always work out like this though, so I will always try and stay calm and maintain respect for those I am working with and not put my stress onto them. I have some good time-management/stress management strategies, so I will try my best to put them into practise.


How will you manage yourself to work productively and safely with other learners, facilitators and industry/community representatives?

I think always remain as calm as possible. As it is a work environment it is important to remain professional and treat the learning environment as a work place. When I am feeling stressed or overwhelmed I will practise the mindfulness exercises we will learn (or the ones I already have) to remain calm and present.


A commitment as to how and where you will seek help in a timely way.

It is important not to put your stress onto others, therefore if I am struggling I will endeavour to:

  • - Not leave work to the last minute so there is time to resolve the situation.
  • - If I have concerns regarding part of the task, I will seek help sooner rather than later.
  • - Follow the correct processes for seeking help, try resolve it myself, google it, ask on slack, then ask facilitator.

Describe what you expect from the facilitation team.

During the Sprints an expectation could be our weekly catch up, and aside from that some assistance or support if I am really struggling with something.


Any scheduling information such as block-out times when you are committed to other things?

I am working 3 days a week until the end of November when my contract finishes. This is Fri, Sat, and Sun. Aside from this I don’t have any external commitments that are firm.
